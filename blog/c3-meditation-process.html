What did you think about mediation before reading chapter 2?

I thought of it as something I ought to investigate more deeply. I have some practice routines which amount to meditation with very measured movement and mindfulness, but I certainly think there is value in investigating meditation as a pursuit with merits beyond my normal activities.

What new things have you learnt about mediation?

I earned that mindfulness meditation trains attention and meta-attention - attention, being conscious of an occurrence or as it is described in a quote by William James, "taking possession by the mind, in clear and vivid form", and meta-attention, consciousness of one's attention paid or the absence thereof. It was also interesting to discover, in Meng's Process Model of Mindfulness Meditation, that an attitude of self criticism lives next to kindness and curiousity. I also think that the concept of a "grandmother mind" is a truly beautiful notion, and it's doubtlessly a great weapon in the battle against neuroses and ailing self-confidence.

Did any of the suggested mediation techniques stand out to you?

Though this is not a technique, I really enjoyed being introduced to the notion of paying meta-attention, because I think it's a very tangible solution to the perpetual drift of my own focus.
